## Intro

Market Data documentation provides reference information about real estate markets, trends, and data sources used in the Everyday Homebuyers workstream. This includes market trends, pricing data, neighborhood information, and data sources for comps analysis and property valuation. Understanding market data enables accurate valuations, informed decisions, and effective strategy.

## Purpose

Centralize market data resources, trends, and reference information to support property analysis, valuation, and strategic decision-making. This ensures consistent use of reliable data sources and market understanding.

## When This Applies

- Analyzing property values
- Understanding market trends
- Making strategic decisions
- Training on market analysis
- Reference during underwriting

## Process / Framework / Workflow

### Market Data Sources

**Primary Sources:**
- MLS (Multiple Listing Service)
- Public records (county assessor, deed records)
- Real estate websites (Zillow, Redfin, Realtor.com)
- Real estate agents and networks
- Appraisal databases

**Secondary Sources:**
- Market reports and analytics
- Economic data
- Demographic information
- Development plans
- Area studies

### Market Trends

**Current Market Conditions:**
- Market type (buyer's/seller's/balanced)
- Inventory levels
- Days on market trends
- Price trends (appreciation/depreciation)
- Interest rate environment

**Historical Trends:**
- Price appreciation over time
- Market cycles
- Seasonal patterns
- Long-term trends
- Market volatility

**Forecasted Trends:**
- Expected market direction
- Economic indicators
- Development impacts
- Demographic changes
- Market predictions

### Neighborhood Data

**Key Neighborhood Factors:**
- School district ratings
- Crime statistics
- Demographics
- Employment data
- Amenities and services
- Future development plans

**Neighborhood Comparison:**
- Comparable neighborhood data
- Price per square foot by area
- Market activity by area
- Buyer preferences by area
- Investment trends by area

### Pricing Data

**Price Ranges:**
- Average sale prices by property type
- Price per square foot by area
- Price ranges by neighborhood
- Luxury vs. standard market
- Investment property pricing

**Pricing Trends:**
- Appreciation rates
- Price volatility
- Seasonal variations
- Market segment trends
- Future price expectations

### Data Quality Standards

**Data Reliability:**
- Use primary sources when possible
- Verify data accuracy
- Cross-reference multiple sources
- Document data sources
- Update data regularly

**Data Usage:**
- Use recent data (prefer <6 months)
- Adjust for market changes
- Consider data limitations
- Apply appropriate methodology
- Document assumptions

## Tools Used

- **MLS Access**: Primary comps data source
- **Public Records**: Property and sale data
- **Real Estate Websites**: Market data and trends
- **Analytics Platforms**: Market analysis tools
- **Data Aggregation Tools**: Compile and analyze data

## Role Responsibilities

**Acquisitions Team**
- Use market data for analysis
- Stay current on market trends
- Apply data appropriately
- Document data sources
- Maintain data quality

**Operations**
- Ensure data source access
- Monitor data quality
- Update market information
- Provide data resources
- Support data usage

## KPIs or SLAs

- **Data Currency**: Market data updated regularly
- **Data Accuracy**: High accuracy in data usage
- **Data Source Diversity**: Multiple sources used
- **Market Understanding**: Team understands market trends

## Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Market Analysis**
Acquisitions team analyzes property. Uses MLS for comps, checks market trends, reviews neighborhood data. Applies market data to valuation. Accurate analysis based on current market data.

**Scenario 2: Market Trend Review**
Team reviews market trends monthly. Updates market data, identifies trends, adjusts strategies. Uses updated data for decision-making. Strategies aligned with market conditions.

**Scenario 3: Data Source Evaluation**
New data source identified. Team evaluates reliability, compares to existing sources, tests accuracy. Integrates if reliable. Data quality maintained, sources optimized.
