## Intro

The Everyday Homebuyers workstream is organized into specialized teams that handle distinct phases of the acquisition and disposition process. This structure enables expertise development, clear accountability, and efficient workflow management. Understanding the organizational structure helps team members know who to contact for specific issues, how work flows between teams, and where their role fits within the broader operation.

## Purpose

Document the organizational structure, reporting relationships, and team responsibilities within the Everyday Homebuyers workstream. This ensures clear communication channels, proper escalation paths, and efficient collaboration.

## When This Applies

- Onboarding new team members
- Understanding reporting relationships
- Identifying who to contact for specific issues
- Planning cross-team collaboration
- Escalating issues or decisions

## Process / Framework / Workflow

### Organizational Structure

**Leadership**
- **COO**: Overall workstream oversight, strategic decisions, process optimization
- **Operations Manager**: Day-to-day operations, workflow coordination, team management

**Lead Management Team**
- **Sales Acquisition Representatives (SARs)**: Lead qualification, initial contact, deal sourcing
- **Lead Management Lead**: SAR team management, process optimization, training

**Acquisitions Team**
- **Acquisitions Specialists**: Underwriting, offer generation, deal negotiation
- **Acquisitions Manager**: Team oversight, deal approval, strategy development

**Dispositions Team**
- **Dispositions Specialists**: Buyer management, deal packet creation, sales execution
- **Dispositions Manager**: Team oversight, buyer list management, sales strategy

**Operations Team**
- **Operations Coordinators**: Workflow management, handoffs, process documentation
- **Automation Specialists**: System integration, workflow automation, tool management

**Support Functions**
- **Training & Development**: Onboarding, ongoing training, performance support
- **Data & Analytics**: Reporting, metrics, performance analysis

### Reporting Relationships

- SARs report to Lead Management Lead
- Acquisitions Specialists report to Acquisitions Manager
- Dispositions Specialists report to Dispositions Manager
- Operations Coordinators report to Operations Manager
- All managers report to COO

### Cross-Team Collaboration

**Lead Management → Acquisitions**
- Qualified leads routed to Acquisitions for underwriting
- SLA: Qualified leads assigned within 1 hour

**Acquisitions → Properties**
- Closed deals handed off to Properties for rehab planning
- Handoff includes property condition report and rehab scope

**Acquisitions → Dispositions**
- Property details and acquisition data shared for disposition planning
- Dispositions team prepares for sale while rehab is in progress

**Dispositions → BuildCo**
- Properties requiring major rehabs or new construction routed to BuildCo
- Coordination on timeline and scope

## Tools Used

- **Org Chart**: Visual representation of structure (maintained in Airtable or HR system)
- **Contact Directory**: Quick reference for team member roles and contact info
- **Escalation Matrix**: Decision-making authority and escalation paths

## Role Responsibilities

**SARs**
- Initial lead contact and qualification
- Data entry and CRM maintenance
- Routing qualified leads to Acquisitions

**Acquisitions Specialists**
- Property underwriting and analysis
- Offer generation and negotiation
- Deal closing coordination

**Dispositions Specialists**
- Buyer communication and relationship management
- Deal packet creation and distribution
- Sales negotiation and closing

**Operations Coordinators**
- Workflow monitoring and optimization
- Cross-team handoff coordination
- Process documentation and improvement

**Managers**
- Team performance management
- Process optimization
- Strategic planning and execution
- Escalation handling

## KPIs or SLAs

- **Response Time**: Managers respond to escalations within 2 hours
- **Handoff Efficiency**: 95% of handoffs completed within SLA timeframes
- **Cross-Team Communication**: Issues resolved within 24 hours
- **Organizational Clarity**: 100% of team members can identify their manager and escalation path

## Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Lead Qualification Question**
SAR encounters an unusual property situation. They escalate to Lead Management Lead, who consults with Acquisitions Manager if needed. Decision is communicated back to SAR within 2 hours.

**Scenario 2: Deal Handoff**
Acquisitions closes a deal and needs to hand off to Properties. Operations Coordinator ensures all required documentation is complete, schedules handoff meeting, and tracks completion.

**Scenario 3: Process Improvement**
Dispositions team identifies a bottleneck. They document the issue, propose a solution, and work with Operations Manager to implement changes that benefit the entire workstream.
