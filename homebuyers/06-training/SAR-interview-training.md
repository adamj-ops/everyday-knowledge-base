## Intro

SAR Interview Training provides guidance for conducting effective interviews with potential Sales Acquisition Representatives. This training ensures consistent, thorough evaluation of candidates, identifies the best fit for the role, and maintains high hiring standards. Effective interview training enables hiring managers to make informed decisions and build a strong team.

## Purpose

Establish standardized interview processes and techniques for evaluating SAR candidates, ensuring consistent evaluation, identifying best-fit candidates, and maintaining hiring quality.

## When This Applies

- Interviewing SAR candidates
- Training hiring managers on interview techniques
- Ensuring consistent interview processes
- Evaluating candidate fit
- Making hiring decisions

## Process / Framework / Workflow

### Interview Structure

**Phone Screen (30 minutes):**
- Initial qualification
- Basic fit assessment
- Schedule in-person interview if qualified

**In-Person Interview (60-90 minutes):**
- Role overview and expectations
- Behavioral questions
- Skills assessment
- Scenario-based questions
- Q&A and candidate questions

**Final Assessment:**
- Reference checks
- Decision and offer
- Onboarding preparation

### Key Interview Areas

**1. Communication Skills**
- Clear and articulate communication
- Professional tone and demeanor
- Active listening
- Ability to build rapport
- Phone and verbal communication

**2. Sales Acumen**
- Understanding of sales process
- Ability to handle objections
- Persistence and resilience
- Goal-oriented mindset
- Results-driven approach

**3. Qualification Skills**
- Ability to ask probing questions
- Listening for key information
- Making qualification decisions
- Handling different scenarios
- Following processes

**4. Cultural Fit**
- Alignment with mission and principles
- Team collaboration
- Adaptability and learning mindset
- Work ethic and reliability
- Positive attitude

**5. Technical Skills**
- CRM and system usage
- Data entry accuracy
- Multitasking ability
- Time management
- Process adherence

### Interview Questions

**Behavioral Questions:**

**Question 1: "Tell me about a time you had to handle a difficult customer or situation. How did you handle it?"**
- **Looking for:** Problem-solving, communication, resilience
- **Good answer:** Shows empathy, problem-solving, positive outcome
- **Red flag:** Blames customer, negative attitude, no resolution

**Question 2: "Describe a time you had to meet a challenging sales or performance goal. How did you achieve it?"**
- **Looking for:** Goal orientation, persistence, results focus
- **Good answer:** Shows planning, effort, achievement
- **Red flag:** No specific examples, excuses, no achievement

**Question 3: "Tell me about a time you had to learn something new quickly. How did you approach it?"**
- **Looking for:** Learning ability, adaptability, initiative
- **Good answer:** Shows proactive learning, resourcefulness
- **Red flag:** Relies on others, slow to learn, resistance

**Scenario-Based Questions:**

**Scenario 1: "A homeowner calls and is upset about an offer. How would you handle this?"**
- **Looking for:** Empathy, problem-solving, professionalism
- **Good answer:** Listen, acknowledge, explain, find solution
- **Red flag:** Defensive, dismissive, no solution

**Scenario 2: "You have 20 new leads to contact today, but you're behind schedule. How do you prioritize?"**
- **Looking for:** Time management, prioritization, process adherence
- **Good answer:** Prioritize by SLA, urgency, value
- **Red flag:** No system, random approach, ignores SLAs

**Scenario 3: "A lead says they're not interested. How do you respond?"**
- **Looking for:** Respect, persistence, qualification skills
- **Good answer:** Respect decision, explore gently, leave door open
- **Red flag:** Pushy, disrespectful, gives up immediately

**Role-Specific Questions:**

**Question 1: "What do you know about our business and this role?"**
- **Looking for:** Research, interest, understanding
- **Good answer:** Shows research, understanding, interest
- **Red flag:** No research, misunderstanding, low interest

**Question 2: "Why are you interested in this role?"**
- **Looking for:** Motivation, fit, alignment
- **Good answer:** Shows genuine interest, alignment with role
- **Red flag:** Generic, no real interest, misalignment

**Question 3: "What are your career goals?"**
- **Looking for:** Ambition, growth mindset, fit
- **Good answer:** Shows growth, alignment with opportunities
- **Red flag:** No goals, misalignment, short-term thinking

### Evaluation Criteria

**Must-Haves:**
- Strong communication skills
- Sales or customer service experience
- Goal-oriented mindset
- Professional demeanor
- Cultural fit

**Nice-to-Haves:**
- Real estate experience
- CRM experience
- Phone sales experience
- High-volume experience
- Bilingual

**Red Flags:**
- Poor communication skills
- Negative attitude
- No sales/customer service experience
- Unreliable or unprofessional
- Misalignment with role/company

### Interview Best Practices

**Preparation:**
- Review candidate resume
- Prepare questions
- Understand role requirements
- Set up interview space
- Review evaluation criteria

**During Interview:**
- Create welcoming environment
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen actively
- Take notes
- Assess fit objectively

**After Interview:**
- Complete evaluation form
- Compare to other candidates
- Check references
- Make decision promptly
- Communicate decision clearly

## KPIs or SLAs

- **Interview Completion**: 100% complete evaluation forms
- **Time to Hire**: Average time from application to offer
- **Hiring Quality**: High performance of hired candidates
- **Candidate Experience**: Positive candidate feedback

## Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Strong Candidate**
Candidate demonstrates excellent communication, sales experience, cultural fit. Answers questions well, shows understanding of role. Reference checks positive. Offer extended, candidate accepts. Successful hire.

**Scenario 2: Borderline Candidate**
Candidate has some skills but gaps in experience. Interview reveals potential but needs development. Decision: hire with extended onboarding, or pass for stronger candidate. Manager evaluates, decides based on team needs.

**Scenario 3: Red Flag Candidate**
Candidate shows poor communication, negative attitude, misalignment. Interview reveals concerns. Decision: pass on candidate. Manager provides feedback, continues search. Avoids bad hire.
