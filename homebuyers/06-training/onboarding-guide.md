## Intro

The Onboarding Guide provides a structured path for new team members joining the Everyday Homebuyers workstream. This guide ensures consistent, comprehensive onboarding that enables new hires to become productive quickly while understanding our processes, tools, and culture. Effective onboarding sets the foundation for long-term success and team integration.

## Purpose

Establish a standardized onboarding process that ensures new team members receive all necessary information, training, and support to succeed in their roles. This accelerates time to productivity and ensures consistent understanding across the team.

## When This Applies

- Onboarding new team members
- Training team members on new processes
- Refreshing team knowledge
- Ensuring consistent onboarding experience

## Process / Framework / Workflow

### Onboarding Timeline

**Week 1: Foundation**
- Day 1: Welcome, introductions, overview
- Day 2-3: System access, tool setup, basic training
- Day 4-5: Process overview, shadowing, initial practice

**Week 2: Deep Dive**
- Role-specific training
- Process deep dives
- Practice and application
- Initial independent work

**Week 3-4: Ramping Up**
- Increased independence
- Performance feedback
- Continued learning
- Integration into team

### Onboarding Checklist

**Pre-Day 1:**
- [ ] Send welcome email with schedule
- [ ] Prepare workspace/equipment
- [ ] Set up system access
- [ ] Prepare training materials
- [ ] Assign onboarding buddy

**Day 1: Welcome & Overview**
- [ ] Welcome meeting with manager
- [ ] Team introductions
- [ ] Workstream overview presentation
- [ ] Mission and principles review
- [ ] Organizational structure overview
- [ ] System access setup
- [ ] Tool access and login setup
- [ ] Review onboarding schedule

**Week 1: Systems & Basics**
- [ ] Airtable CRM training
- [ ] PhoneBurner setup and training
- [ ] Email and SMS platform training
- [ ] Communication tools setup
- [ ] Process overview sessions
- [ ] Shadow experienced team member
- [ ] Complete initial practice exercises

**Week 2: Role-Specific Training**
- [ ] Role expectations and responsibilities
- [ ] Detailed process training
- [ ] Script and template training
- [ ] Quality standards training
- [ ] Performance metrics overview
- [ ] Practice with supervision
- [ ] Initial feedback sessions

**Week 3-4: Integration**
- [ ] Independent work with support
- [ ] Regular check-ins with manager
- [ ] Performance review and feedback
- [ ] Continued learning and development
- [ ] Team integration activities
- [ ] 30-day review meeting

## Role Responsibilities

**New Team Members**
- Complete onboarding checklist
- Attend all training sessions
- Ask questions and seek help
- Practice and apply learning
- Provide feedback on onboarding

**Managers**
- Oversee onboarding process
- Provide role-specific training
- Monitor progress and provide feedback
- Ensure successful integration
- Address issues promptly

**Onboarding Buddy**
- Provide peer support
- Answer questions
- Share knowledge and experience
- Help with integration
- Provide feedback

**Operations**
- Coordinate onboarding logistics
- Ensure consistent experience
- Monitor onboarding effectiveness
- Optimize onboarding process
- Maintain onboarding materials

## KPIs or SLAs

- **Onboarding Completion**: 100% complete within 4 weeks
- **Time to Productivity**: 80% productive within 4 weeks
- **Onboarding Satisfaction**: High satisfaction scores
- **Retention Rate**: High retention of onboarded team members

## Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Standard Onboarding**
New SAR joins team. Completes Day 1 welcome, Week 1 systems training, Week 2 role-specific training. Practices with supervision, receives feedback. By Week 4, working independently, meeting performance targets. Successful onboarding.

**Scenario 2: Accelerated Onboarding**
Experienced hire joins team. Has relevant experience, accelerates through basics. Focuses on role-specific training and team integration. Reaches productivity faster. Onboarding adapted to experience level.

**Scenario 3: Extended Onboarding**
New team member needs additional support. Onboarding extended, additional training provided, more practice time. Manager and buddy provide extra support. Eventually reaches productivity. Onboarding adapted to individual needs.
