## Intro

SMS templates provide standardized, effective text messages for common communication scenarios with leads and homeowners. Text messaging is often the preferred communication method for many homeowners, offering convenience and rapid response. These templates ensure consistent messaging while allowing for personalization and adaptation to specific situations.

## Purpose

Establish a library of SMS templates that SARs can use for common scenarios, ensuring consistent, professional communication while saving time and maintaining quality. Templates are designed to be personalized and adapted as needed.

## When This Applies

- Initial contact with new leads
- Follow-up communications
- Scheduling calls or meetings
- Providing information or updates
- Re-engaging leads
- Confirming appointments or details

## Process / Framework / Workflow

### SMS Best Practices

**Tone:**
- Professional but friendly
- Concise and clear
- Respectful of their time
- Personal (use their name)

**Length:**
- Keep messages under 160 characters when possible
- Break longer messages into multiple texts if needed
- Get to the point quickly

**Timing:**
- Business hours preferred (9 AM - 8 PM)
- Avoid early morning or late evening
- Respect "do not text" preferences

**Personalization:**
- Always use their name
- Reference specific property or situation
- Adapt template to conversation context

### Template Categories

**Initial Contact Templates**

**Template 1: First Contact - Inbound Lead**
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Everyday Homebuyers. I saw your inquiry about [Property Address]. Do you have a few minutes for a quick call? I'd love to see how we can help. [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 2: First Contact - Outbound Lead**
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Everyday Homebuyers. We help homeowners sell properties quickly for cash. I noticed you own [Property Address]. Is this something you've ever considered? Happy to chat if interested. [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 3: First Contact - Referral**
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Everyday Homebuyers. [Referrer Name] thought you might be interested in learning about our cash home buying service. Would you be open to a quick conversation? [Your Phone Number]"

**Follow-Up Templates**

**Template 4: Follow-Up After No Response**
"Hi [Name], I tried reaching you earlier about [Property Address]. I know you're busy - would a quick text work better? We buy houses for cash and can close in 7-14 days. Interested in learning more? [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 5: Follow-Up After Voicemail**
"Hi [Name], I left you a voicemail about [Property Address]. We help homeowners sell quickly for cash - no repairs needed. Would love to chat when convenient. [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 6: Follow-Up - Scheduled**
"Hi [Name], just confirming our call scheduled for [Time/Date] about [Property Address]. Looking forward to chatting! If you need to reschedule, just let me know. [Your Phone Number]"

**Information Templates**

**Template 7: Sending Information**
"Hi [Name], as promised, here's some information about our cash home buying process: [Link or Brief Info]. Happy to answer any questions. [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 8: Offer Update**
"Hi [Name], great news! We've completed our analysis of [Property Address] and have an offer ready. When's a good time to discuss? [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 9: Next Steps**
"Hi [Name], next step is [Action]. I'll [What You'll Do]. You'll need to [What They Need to Do]. Questions? Just text or call. [Your Phone Number]"

**Re-Engagement Templates**

**Template 10: Re-Engage Old Lead**
"Hi [Name], I know we talked a while back about [Property Address]. Wanted to check in - is selling still something you're considering? We're still buying in your area. [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 11: Re-Engage After "Not Ready"**
"Hi [Name], I know you mentioned you weren't ready to sell when we last talked. Just checking in - has your situation changed? We're here when you're ready. [Your Phone Number]"

**Scheduling Templates**

**Template 12: Request Call**
"Hi [Name], would [Day/Time] work for a quick call about [Property Address]? If not, let me know what works better. [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 13: Confirm Appointment**
"Hi [Name], confirming our [Call/Meeting] scheduled for [Day/Time] about [Property Address]. Talk soon! [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 14: Reschedule Request**
"Hi [Name], something came up - can we reschedule our call to [New Day/Time]? Let me know if that works. [Your Phone Number]"

**Foreclosure-Specific Templates**

**Template 15: Foreclosure Initial Contact**
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Everyday Homebuyers. I understand you may be facing challenges with your mortgage. We might be able to help you avoid foreclosure with a quick cash sale. Would you be open to a brief conversation? [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 16: Foreclosure Follow-Up**
"Hi [Name], I know foreclosure situations are stressful. We can often close in 7-14 days, which might help you avoid the foreclosure sale. Would you like to explore this option? [Your Phone Number]"

**Investor-Specific Templates**

**Template 17: Investor Initial Contact**
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Everyday Homebuyers. We work with investors to buy properties quickly for cash. I noticed you own [Property Address]. Interested in a quick conversation? [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 18: Investor Follow-Up**
"Hi [Name], following up about [Property Address]. We can close in 7-14 days and free up your capital quickly. Worth exploring? [Your Phone Number]"

**Closing Templates**

**Template 19: Thank You**
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to talk today about [Property Address]. I'll [Next Action] and get back to you by [Timeline]. Have a great day! [Your Phone Number]"

**Template 20: Deal Closing**
"Hi [Name], congratulations! We're moving forward with [Property Address]. Next steps: [Brief Summary]. I'll keep you updated. Excited to work with you! [Your Phone Number]"

### Template Usage Guidelines

**Personalization:**
- Always replace [Placeholders] with actual information
- Add personal touches based on conversation
- Reference specific details from previous interactions

**Adaptation:**
- Templates are starting points, not scripts
- Adapt tone and content to situation
- Add context or details as needed

**Timing:**
- Send during business hours
- Respect response times
- Don't over-text (max 2-3 per day)

**Response Handling:**
- Respond to texts promptly (< 1 hour)
- If they prefer text, continue via text
- If complex, offer to call instead

## Tools Used

- **SMS Platform**: Text messaging system with template library
- **CRM Integration**: Link SMS to lead records
- **Template Library**: Centralized template database
- **Response Tracking**: Monitor response rates by template

## Role Responsibilities

**SARs**
- Use templates as starting points
- Personalize templates appropriately
- Adapt templates to specific situations
- Track which templates work best
- Suggest new templates as needed

**Lead Management Lead**
- Maintain template library
- Review template effectiveness
- Update templates based on performance
- Train team on template usage

## KPIs or SLAs

- **SMS Response Rate**: 40%+ response rate on initial texts
- **Response Time**: Respond to SMS within 1 hour
- **Template Effectiveness**: Track open rates and responses by template
- **SMS Conversion**: % of SMS leads that qualify

## Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Inbound Lead SMS**
Homeowner submits web form. SAR sends Template 1 within 5 minutes. Homeowner responds immediately, prefers text. SAR continues conversation via text, qualifies, schedules call.

**Scenario 2: Follow-Up After No Answer**
SAR calls lead, no answer, leaves voicemail. Sends Template 5 via SMS 30 minutes later. Homeowner responds via text, prefers text communication. SAR continues via text.

**Scenario 3: Foreclosure Lead**
Lead identified as foreclosure situation. SAR sends Template 15, acknowledges difficulty, offers help. Homeowner responds, very interested. SAR uses foreclosure script via phone, qualifies, routes.
