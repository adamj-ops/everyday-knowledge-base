## Intro

Foreclosure situations require specialized, sensitive communication that acknowledges the homeowner's difficult circumstances while offering a potential solution. This script provides guidance for handling foreclosure leads with empathy, professionalism, and effectiveness. The approach balances compassion with business objectives, ensuring we can help homeowners while maintaining deal viability.

## Purpose

Provide a specialized script and framework for handling foreclosure leads with appropriate sensitivity, empathy, and effectiveness. This ensures we can help homeowners in difficult situations while maintaining professional standards and deal viability.

## When This Applies

- Contacting leads identified as being in foreclosure
- Homeowners mention foreclosure during qualification
- Re-engaging leads who previously mentioned foreclosure concerns
- Training team members on sensitive situation handling

## Process / Framework / Workflow

### Foreclosure Situation Understanding

**Common Foreclosure Scenarios:**
- Pre-foreclosure: Behind on payments, notice received
- Auction scheduled: Sale date set, limited time
- Post-foreclosure: Already lost property, redemption period
- Loan modification attempts: Tried to work with lender, unsuccessful

**Emotional Considerations:**
- High stress and anxiety
- Embarrassment or shame
- Fear of losing home
- Financial pressure
- Family impact

### Script Approach

**Tone Guidelines:**
- **Empathetic**: Acknowledge difficulty of situation
- **Non-judgmental**: Avoid language that implies fault
- **Solution-focused**: Emphasize how we can help
- **Respectful**: Treat with dignity and respect
- **Urgent but calm**: Convey time sensitivity without panic

### Foreclosure-Specific Script

**Opening:**
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Everyday Homebuyers. I'm calling because I understand you may be facing some challenges with your mortgage situation. I know this can be really stressful, and I wanted to reach out to see if we might be able to help. Is now an okay time to talk?"

**Acknowledgment:**
"I can only imagine how stressful this situation must be. Losing a home is one of the most difficult things anyone can go through. The good news is, you still have options, and we might be able to help you avoid foreclosure and get a fresh start."

**Value Proposition:**
"We buy houses quickly for cash, which means we can often close in 7-14 days - much faster than a traditional sale. This can help you avoid foreclosure, protect your credit, and give you cash to move forward. We handle all the details, so you don't have to worry about repairs or staging."

**Qualification Questions (Sensitive Approach):**
- "Can you tell me a bit about your situation? I want to make sure I understand so I can help in the best way possible."
- "What's your timeline looking like? When is the foreclosure sale scheduled, if you don't mind me asking?"
- "Have you been able to work with your lender at all, or is the sale date already set?"
- "What would be most helpful for you right now - avoiding the foreclosure, getting cash quickly, or both?"

**Solution Presentation:**
"Based on what you've shared, here's how we might be able to help: We can make you a cash offer on your property. If you accept, we can close quickly - often before the foreclosure sale date. This would help you avoid the foreclosure on your record, give you cash to move forward, and let you walk away clean. Would you like to see what we could offer?"

**Objection Handling:**

**"I don't want to lose my home"**
- "I completely understand - this is your home, and that's really hard. The reality is, if the foreclosure goes through, you'll lose it anyway and won't get anything. By selling to us, you can at least get cash and avoid the foreclosure on your credit. It's not the outcome you wanted, but it might be the best option available."

**"Your offer will be too low"**
- "I understand that concern. We base our offers on the current market value. I know it might be less than what you hoped, but consider: if the foreclosure goes through, you'll get nothing and still have the foreclosure on your record. Our offer gives you cash and a clean exit. Would you like to see the numbers?"

**"I'm trying to work with my lender"**
- "That's great that you're trying to work with them. Many homeowners find that lenders are difficult to work with, especially if you're already behind. Our offer gives you a backup plan. You can continue working with your lender, but if that doesn't work out, you'll have our offer ready. Does that make sense?"

**"I need more time to think"**
- "I completely understand you need time to think. The challenge is, foreclosure timelines don't wait. Can I ask - what questions can I answer to help you make a decision? Or would it help if I sent you some information you can review?"

### Special Considerations

**Time Sensitivity:**
- Foreclosure sales have hard deadlines
- Emphasize speed and urgency appropriately
- Don't create false urgency, but be clear about real deadlines

**Legal Awareness:**
- We are not attorneys or legal advisors
- Don't provide legal advice
- Refer to attorneys when appropriate
- Focus on our service: buying properties

**Credit Impact:**
- Selling to us avoids foreclosure on credit report
- Foreclosure stays on credit 7 years
- Short sale also impacts credit but less severely
- Emphasize credit protection benefit

**Financial Reality:**
- Many homeowners have little or no equity
- Our offer reflects market value minus repairs
- Be honest about likely offer range
- Set realistic expectations

## Role Responsibilities

**SARs**
- Use foreclosure script with appropriate sensitivity
- Acknowledge emotional difficulty
- Provide clear information about options
- Route qualified leads promptly (time-sensitive)
- Escalate complex situations

**Lead Management Lead**
- Train team on foreclosure sensitivity
- Review calls for appropriate tone
- Provide support for difficult conversations
- Monitor foreclosure lead conversion rates

## KPIs or SLAs

- **Foreclosure Lead Response**: < 2 hours (more urgent than standard)
- **Conversion Rate**: Target 40%+ (higher motivation)
- **Time to Offer**: 24 hours (expedited due to urgency)
- **Homeowner Satisfaction**: High marks for empathy and professionalism

## Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Pre-Foreclosure Lead**
Homeowner received notice, sale scheduled in 45 days. SAR uses foreclosure script, acknowledges stress, explains how quick sale can help. Homeowner qualifies, understands urgency. Lead routed immediately, Acquisitions prioritizes, offer presented within 24 hours.

**Scenario 2: Already in Foreclosure**
Sale date in 2 weeks, homeowner desperate. SAR uses script, emphasizes speed, explains credit protection benefit. Homeowner qualifies, very motivated. Expedited process, offer and acceptance within 3 days, closing scheduled before sale date.

**Scenario 3: Emotional Resistance**
Homeowner in foreclosure but emotionally attached, resistant to selling. SAR uses empathetic approach, acknowledges difficulty, explains reality of situation, offers solution without pressure. Homeowner needs time, SAR schedules follow-up, provides information.
