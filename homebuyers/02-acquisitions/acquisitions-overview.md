## Intro

The Acquisitions function is responsible for evaluating qualified leads, determining property values, calculating repair costs, generating offers, and negotiating deals to successful closing. This team transforms qualified opportunities into closed acquisitions by applying systematic underwriting processes, market analysis, and strategic negotiation. Acquisitions is the critical bridge between lead generation and property ownership, ensuring we acquire properties at prices that enable profitable dispositions.

## Purpose

Provide a comprehensive overview of the acquisitions function, including processes, responsibilities, tools, and how acquisitions integrates with other workstream functions. This serves as the foundation for understanding how deals are evaluated, priced, and closed.

## When This Applies

- Understanding the acquisitions process
- Onboarding new acquisitions team members
- Analyzing deal performance and conversion rates
- Optimizing underwriting and offer strategies
- Coordinating handoffs from Lead Management

## Process / Framework / Workflow

### Acquisitions Workflow

1. **Lead Receipt**: Receive qualified lead from Lead Management team
2. **Initial Review**: Quick assessment of opportunity viability
3. **Underwriting**: Comprehensive property analysis and valuation
4. **Offer Generation**: Calculate and prepare offer based on underwriting
5. **Offer Presentation**: Present offer to homeowner with explanation
6. **Negotiation**: Handle counter-offers and term negotiations
7. **Acceptance**: Finalize terms and prepare for closing
8. **Closing Coordination**: Work with title company, attorneys, and operations
9. **Handoff**: Transfer property to Properties team for rehab

### Key Activities

**Underwriting:**
- Comps analysis for ARV determination
- Repair cost estimation
- MAO (Maximum Allowable Offer) calculation
- Deal viability assessment

**Offer Strategy:**
- Initial offer positioning
- Negotiation room planning
- Terms and conditions development
- Risk assessment

**Deal Management:**
- Communication with homeowners
- Negotiation execution
- Closing coordination
- Documentation management

## Tools Used

- **Airtable CRM**: Deal tracking, pipeline management, data storage
- **Comps Analysis Tools**: MLS access, public records, valuation software
- **ARV Calculators**: Automated valuation models and spreadsheets
- **Repair Estimators**: Cost databases, contractor quotes, historical data
- **Deal Analyzer**: Comprehensive underwriting spreadsheet
- **Title Companies**: Title search and closing coordination
- **Attorneys**: Legal review and closing support

## Role Responsibilities

**Acquisitions Specialists**
- Receive and review qualified leads
- Complete underwriting analysis
- Generate offers based on analysis
- Present offers to homeowners
- Negotiate terms and conditions
- Coordinate closing process
- Hand off closed deals to Properties

**Acquisitions Manager**
- Oversee acquisitions team performance
- Review and approve offers above certain thresholds
- Provide underwriting support and guidance
- Optimize processes and strategies
- Manage relationships with title companies and attorneys
- Analyze deal performance and conversion rates

## KPIs or SLAs

- **Underwriting Time**: 24-48 hours for standard properties
- **Offer Generation**: Offers generated within 4 hours of underwriting completion
- **Offer Acceptance Rate**: 15-20% of offers accepted
- **Negotiation Success**: 60%+ of accepted offers close successfully
- **Closing Timeline**: 14-30 days from acceptance to closing
- **Deal Profitability**: Average profit margin meets target thresholds

## Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Standard Acquisition**
Acquisitions receives qualified lead. Specialist completes underwriting: runs comps, estimates $25k in repairs, calculates ARV of $180k, determines MAO of $120k. Generates offer of $115k, presents to homeowner. Homeowner counters at $125k. After negotiation, agree at $120k. Deal closes in 21 days, handed off to Properties.

**Scenario 2: Competitive Situation**
Multiple offers on property. Acquisitions determines property is high-value opportunity. Adjusts offer strategy, presents strong offer quickly. Homeowner accepts. Fast-track closing in 14 days to secure deal.

**Scenario 3: Complex Underwriting**
Property has unique features, limited comps. Acquisitions uses alternative valuation methods, consults with manager, gets contractor quote for unusual repairs. Completes thorough underwriting, generates conservative offer. Homeowner accepts after brief negotiation.
