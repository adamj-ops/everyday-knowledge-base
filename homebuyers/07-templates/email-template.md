## Intro

The Email Template provides a standardized format for creating professional, effective emails used throughout the Everyday Homebuyers workstream. This template ensures consistent communication, proper structure, and effective messaging while allowing for personalization. Using this template enables efficient email creation and professional communication.

## Purpose

Provide a reusable template for email communication that ensures consistent structure, professional tone, and effective messaging. This enables efficient email creation and consistent communication quality.

## When This Applies

- Creating new email templates
- Standardizing email communication
- Training on email best practices
- Improving email effectiveness
- Ensuring professional communication

## Process / Framework / Workflow

### Email Template Structure

**Email Header:**
- Subject Line
- To/CC/BCC
- Priority Level

**Email Body:**
1. Greeting
2. Opening/Context
3. Main Message
4. Call to Action
5. Closing
6. Signature

### Email Components

**Subject Line:**
- Clear and descriptive
- Action-oriented when appropriate
- Include key information
- Keep concise (<50 characters ideal)

**Greeting:**
- Personalized (use recipient's name)
- Professional tone
- Appropriate formality level

**Opening:**
- Context setting
- Reason for email
- Brief introduction if needed

**Main Message:**
- Clear and concise
- Well-organized (bullets, paragraphs)
- Key information highlighted
- Professional tone

**Call to Action:**
- Clear next steps
- Specific requests
- Timeline if applicable
- Easy to respond

**Closing:**
- Professional sign-off
- Contact information
- Additional resources if relevant

**Signature:**
- Name
- Title
- Company
- Contact information
- Professional formatting

### Email Template Examples

**Template 1: Initial Property Outreach**

**Subject:** New Property Opportunity: [Property Address]

Hi [Buyer Name],

I have a new property that matches your buying criteria and wanted to share it with you first.

**Property Highlights:**
- Address: [Address]
- Type: [Property Type]
- Size: [Square Footage], [Bedrooms]BR/[Bathrooms]BA
- Asking Price: $[Price]
- Estimated ARV: $[ARV]
- Estimated ROI: [Percentage]%

**Why This Property:**
- [Key Selling Point 1]
- [Key Selling Point 2]
- [Key Selling Point 3]

I've attached a complete deal packet with photos, inspection reports, and financial projections. This property [specific opportunity].

Would you like to schedule a showing? I'm available [times]. Let me know what works for you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Title]
Everyday Homebuyers
[Phone Number]
[Email]

---

**Template 2: Follow-Up**

**Subject:** Following Up: [Property Address]

Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on [topic] I sent you [timeframe]. Have you had a chance to [action]?

[Brief reminder of key points]

[Next steps or request]

Would you like to [action]? I'm available [times].

Best regards,
[Your Name]

---

**Template 3: Offer Response**

**Subject:** Response: Your Offer on [Property Address]

Hi [Buyer Name],

Thanks for your offer on [Property Address]. I've reviewed it with the team.

[Acceptance/Counter/Rejection message]

[If Accepted:]
Great news! We've accepted your offer. I'll send over the purchase agreement shortly.

[If Counter:]
We'd like to counter with the following:
- Price: $[Amount]
- Terms: [Terms]

[If Rejected:]
Unfortunately, we've accepted another offer. Thank you for your interest, and I'll keep you in mind for future properties.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

### Email Best Practices

**Structure:**
- Use clear subject lines
- Keep emails concise
- Use bullets for lists
- Include clear call to action
- Professional formatting

**Tone:**
- Professional but friendly
- Clear and direct
- Respectful
- Appropriate formality
- Personal when appropriate

**Content:**
- Get to point quickly
- Include necessary information
- Avoid unnecessary details
- Use clear language
- Proofread before sending

**Timing:**
- Send during business hours
- Respond promptly (<2 hours)
- Follow up appropriately
- Don't over-communicate
