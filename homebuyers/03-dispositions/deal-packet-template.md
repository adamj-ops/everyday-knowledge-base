## Intro

The Deal Packet Template provides a standardized format for presenting properties to cash buyers. A well-crafted deal packet includes all information buyers need to evaluate and make offers, presented in a clear, professional format. Effective deal packets increase buyer interest, generate more offers, and enable faster sales at better prices.

## Purpose

Establish a comprehensive, standardized deal packet template that ensures all necessary information is included and presented effectively. This enables consistent, professional property presentations and maximizes buyer engagement.

## When This Applies

- Creating deal packets for properties ready for sale
- Training dispositions team on packet creation
- Ensuring consistency across all property presentations
- Optimizing deal packet effectiveness

## Process / Framework / Workflow

### Deal Packet Structure

**Section 1: Property Overview**
- Property address
- Property type (single family, multi-family, etc.)
- Square footage
- Bedrooms and bathrooms
- Lot size
- Year built
- Property condition summary

**Section 2: Property Photos**
- Exterior photos (front, back, sides)
- Interior photos (all rooms, key features)
- Property condition photos (repairs needed, etc.)
- Neighborhood/area photos (if relevant)

**Section 3: Property Details**
- Detailed property description
- Key features and amenities
- Recent updates or improvements
- Property condition details
- Repair needs and scope

**Section 4: Financial Information**
- Asking price
- Estimated ARV (After Repair Value)
- Estimated repair costs
- Estimated holding costs
- Projected profit potential
- ROI calculations

**Section 5: Market Analysis**
- Comparable sales (comps)
- Market trends
- Neighborhood information
- Area demographics
- Future development plans (if relevant)

**Section 6: Inspection Reports**
- Property inspection report (if available)
- Repair estimates
- Contractor quotes (if available)
- Property condition assessment

**Section 7: Terms & Conditions**
- Purchase terms
- Closing timeline
- Contingencies (if any)
- Property access for showings
- Contact information

### Deal Packet Best Practices

**Content Quality:**
- Include all relevant information
- Be honest about property condition
- Highlight opportunities, not just problems
- Provide accurate financial projections
- Use high-quality photos

**Presentation:**
- Professional formatting
- Clear organization
- Easy to read and navigate
- Consistent branding
- Error-free (proofread!)

**Completeness:**
- All sections completed
- No missing information
- Supporting documents included
- Contact information clear

**Effectiveness:**
- Compelling but accurate
- Emphasizes value and opportunity
- Addresses buyer concerns proactively
- Makes it easy to take next step

## Tools Used

- **Deal Packet Template**: This template (digital or printed)
- **Photo Management**: Property photos and organization
- **Financial Calculators**: ROI and profit calculations
- **Document Creation**: Word, PDF, or presentation software
- **CRM System**: Store and distribute deal packets

## Role Responsibilities

**Dispositions Specialists**
- Create deal packets using template
- Ensure all sections completed
- Include high-quality photos
- Verify financial information accuracy
- Review for completeness before distribution

**Dispositions Manager**
- Review deal packet quality
- Provide feedback and improvements
- Ensure template consistency
- Optimize template based on results
- Train team on template usage

## KPIs or SLAs

- **Deal Packet Completion**: Within 48 hours of property ready
- **Deal Packet Quality**: 95%+ include all required sections
- **Buyer Response Rate**: 40%+ response rate on deal packets
- **Offer Generation**: Average 3-5 offers per deal packet

## Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Standard Deal Packet**
Property ready for sale. Dispositions creates deal packet using template: includes all sections, 15 photos, financial breakdown, comps analysis. Reviews for completeness, distributes to 15 buyers. 8 buyers respond, 5 schedule showings, receive 4 offers.

**Scenario 2: Quick Deal Packet**
High-demand property, need to move quickly. Dispositions creates streamlined deal packet: essential information, key photos, basic financials. Distributes immediately. Receives multiple offers within days.

**Scenario 3: Complex Property**
Property has unique features, requires detailed explanation. Dispositions creates comprehensive deal packet: detailed description, extensive photos, thorough financial analysis, market positioning. Helps buyers understand opportunity. Eventually receives acceptable offers.
